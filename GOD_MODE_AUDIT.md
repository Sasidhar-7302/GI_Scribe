## Case: GAS0001
**Patient**: 32yo Female (8w Pregnancy)

### History of Present Illness (HPI)
Patient is a 32-year-old female, G1P0, currently at 8 weeks gestation, presenting for evaluation of nausea and vomiting of pregnancy. Symptoms began approximately 9 days ago and have been persistent throughout the day, though slightly worse in the mornings. She reports vomiting 1-2 times per day and identifies specific food triggers. She also reports increased urinary frequency every 1-2 hours and increased thirst. She denies abdominal pain, fever, chills, or changes in bowel habits. She has been trying ginger supplements with minimal relief.

### Assessment & Evidence
1. **Nausea and vomiting of pregnancy (NVP)** - supported by 9-day history of persistent symptoms and vomiting.
2. **Increased urinary frequency** - supported by patient report of going every 1-2 hours.
3. **Increased thirst** - supported by patient report.

## Case: GAS0002
**Case Type**: Symptom-based consultation

### History of Present Illness (HPI)
The patient presents for evaluation of dysuria and increased urinary frequency over the past three days. Symptoms include a burning sensation during urination and a frequent urge to void. The patient denies flank pain, fever, or hematuria. No previous history of similar urinary tract symptoms.

### Assessment & Evidence
1. **Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)** - supported by 3-day history of dysuria and increased frequency.
2. **Cystitis** - supported by localized urinary symptoms without systemic involvement (fever/flank pain).
